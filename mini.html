  Physical Description

  Lifespan/Longevity   

Range lifespan             Typical lifespan
Status: captivity           Status: captivity
3 (high) years              1 to 3 years


  Food Habits   

Primary Diet: carnivore (eats non-insect arthropods); omnivore
Animal Foods: amphibians; fish; insects; aquatic crustaceans
Plant Foods: leaves; fruit; macroalgae


  Predation   

  There is little information available regarding predators specific to Chironectes minimus. Tortato (2009)   reported a single predation event by a roadside hawk in Brazil. Chironectes minimus also has been   reported in the diet of large eagles such as hawk-eagles; however, these birds are relatively rare within   this habitat and likely have little impact on the overall population. Wild cats, such as ocelots, jaguars,   pumas and jaguarundis likely prey on C. minimus as well. Its nocturnal lifestyle and burrowing   tendencies likely help reduce its risk of predation.
